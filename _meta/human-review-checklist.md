# Human Review Checklist

Use this checklist before promoting a page from `draft` to `reviewed` or
`published`.

## Accuracy

- [ ] Technical claims are correct.
- [ ] Product-specific claims have references.
- [ ] Version-specific claims are current or clearly dated.
- [ ] Examples match the described behavior.
- [ ] Ambiguous claims are rewritten or removed.

## Safety

- [ ] No secrets or private data.
- [ ] No real internal hostnames or customer details.
- [ ] No weaponized exploit steps.
- [ ] No stealth, evasion, persistence, or bypass guidance.
- [ ] Red-team content is authorization-first.

## Usefulness

- [ ] Reader knows what to do next.
- [ ] Examples are practical.
- [ ] Detection content includes false positives.
- [ ] Playbooks include evidence and containment.
- [ ] Architecture pages include failure modes.

## Writing Quality

- [ ] No generic filler.
- [ ] No repetitive AI-like phrasing.
- [ ] Paragraphs are concise.
- [ ] Tables are used where helpful.
- [ ] The page sounds like an experienced practitioner wrote it.

## Maintenance

- [ ] Owner or domain is clear.
- [ ] Review date is clear where needed.
- [ ] References are not stale.
- [ ] Related pages are linked.
